Thesis & Capstone ‚ñº

Health Informatics for Underserved Communities

Role: Research Lead ‚Ä¢ Timeline: Jan 2024 - Dec 2025

Honors Thesis ‚Ä¢ Indy Local Collective

Research Question: How can health informatics solutions informed by public health principles, prevention strategies, and stress management approaches improve healthcare access and technology adoption for underserved communities?

Methods: Participatory workshops (n=15), semi-structured interviews, usability testing (3 rounds), epidemiological analysis and thematic analysis synthesizing 150+ data points

Participants: 15+ local organizations across healthcare, community services, and social services

Key Findings: (1) Social determinants of health (time, resources, access) were bigger barriers than technical literacy; (2) Prevention-focused approaches and stress management considerations were essential for sustainable health technology adoption; (3) Trust-building through community connections was critical for health intervention success; (4) Mobile-first health solutions addressing prevention and stress reduction were most effective for underserved populations

Participatory Research Accessibility Thematic Analysis

Impact: Health technology framework adopted by local organizations; informed community health programs; presented findings at IU Leadership Conference

Human-Centered AI Consulting Framework

Role: Human-centered AI Researcher ‚Ä¢ Timeline: Jan 2024 - Dec 2025

Capstone Project ‚Ä¢ Matcha Green Consulting

Research Question: What are the barriers preventing micro-businesses from conducting user research?

Methods: Case study analysis (n=8), in-depth interviews, journey mapping workshops, framework validation with 3 businesses

Participants: 8 micro-business entrepreneurs across food service, retail, and professional services

Key Findings: (1) Cost and time were the biggest barriers; (2) Businesses needed templates and step-by-step guides; (3) Research needed to be framed as "customer feedback" to reduce intimidation; (4) Peer learning and community support were essential

Impact: Honorable mention at IU Leadership Conference; businesses used the toolkit to conduct their first user research
